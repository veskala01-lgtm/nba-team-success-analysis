# Evaluating the Impact of Defensive Efficiency on NBA Team Success (2025-2026 Season)

An empirical analysis of whether defensive performance 
is as influential as offensive performance in determining 
NBA team success, inspired by Victor Wembanyama's claim 
that "defense is 50% of the game."

## Research Question
To what extent does defensive performance contribute to 
NBA team success, and is it as influential as offensive 
performance?

## Key Findings
- Offensive Rating alone explains 79.3% of win percentage variance (R² = 0.793)
- Defensive Rating alone explains 78.7% of win percentage variance (R² = 0.787)  
- Combined model explains 94.2% of variance (R² = 0.942)
- Net Rating rankings correlate with actual standings at r = 0.95 (Spearman)
- Notable outliers: Lakers outperformed efficiency metrics due to clutch performance; 
  Hornets underperformed despite strong efficiency

## Conclusion
The data supports Wembanyama's intuition: defense and offense 
contribute nearly equally to winning. Neither dominates alone, 
but their combination is a near-complete predictor of team success.
